Partnership Firm

Partnership firm is a business entity created by persons who have agreed to share profit or loss of business. Partnership is a good choice of business entity for small enterprises wherein two or more persons decides to contribute to a business and share the profit or loss. In India, Partnership are widely prevalent because of its ease of formation and minimal regulatory compliance. Hence, Partnership are most prevalent type of business entity wherein a group of people are involved.

One Person Company

The concept of One Person Company in India introduced through Companies Act, 2013 to support entrepreneurs who on their own capable of starting venture by allowing them to create a single person business entity. One of the biggest advantages of One Person Company (OPC) is that there can be only one member in OPC, while a minimum of two members required for incorporating and maintaining Private Limited Company or Limited Liability Partnership (LLP). Similar to a Private Limited Company, a One Person Company is a separate legal entity from its promoter, offering limited liability protection to its sole shareholder, while having continuity of business and being easy to incorporate.
